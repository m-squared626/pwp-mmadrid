About Me

Born and raised in the shadow of the Sangre de Christo Mountains between Santa Fe and Las Vegas New Mexico along the Pecos River. I grew up working on the family ranch assisting my family with everyday life growing the family business attending to hundreds of cattle. Working in the family business I learned the value of an honest days work where nothing is taken for granted. It's where I developed my exceptional work ethic and determination to do my best, continue learning, and striving to live my best life.

I attended West Las Vegas High School where I exceled in sports and academics. After graduation I moved to Las Cruces, NM to attend New Mexico State University (NMSU). While at NMSU I double majored achieving a Bachelor of Science Degree in Mechanical Engineering Technology and a Bachelor of Business Administration in Finance. During my years of college I began my career working summers with the New Mexico Highway and Transformation Department, first as a lab tech, then in the field monitoring roadway and bridge construction projects.

I began my engineering career when I accepted a Mechanical Engineering position with Johnson & Johnson Medical Inc. (J&J) in El Paso Texas my senior year. Working in El Paso also required working in Mexico briefly at the manufacturing facilities owned by J&J exposing me to international experience. This was followed by me accepting a position with McNeil Consumer products, a J&J company, in Austin, TX as a Manufacturing/Mechanical Engineer.

When an opportunity presented itself with the possibility of returning to New Mexico to be closer to family I quickly accepted a position as a manufacturing technician with Intel Corp., in Rio Rancho, NM. While at Intel I was quickly promoted to a process control manufacturing technician in the high tech manufacturing environment, which is usually reserved for more senior technicians. My education, previous work, experience, hard work, and dedication allowed me to achieve numerous promotions which lead me to accept an Industrial/Manufacturing Engineering position. I exceled to become a senior Industrial Engineer responsible for some of the most expensive high tech equipment used to manufacture semiconductors. I collaborated with my domestic and international peers to improve tool performance optimizing factory layout space and utilizing equipment from a cost basis.

After some time away from the work industry, which allowed me to spend quality time with family and friends, travel extensively, and pursue other interests I'm ready to get back to work and rejoin the work environment. To make sure my skills are up to date I enrolled at CNM Ingenuity Inc,. Deep Dive Boot-camp Full stack Web Development course. Both to update my technical skills and pursue another interest of learning to code in the full stack web development program.
